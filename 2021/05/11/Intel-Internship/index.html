Intel Internship

Posted on 2021-05-11 Edited on 2021-12-09 Disqus:

Intel is probably the one of the most historical and famous tech company there is. I grew up listening to intel boot-up sounds and peeling the “intel inside” stickers on laptops and desktops alike. While it’s not the most prestigious software engineer destination nor the class-leading innovator now, I’m a big fan of Intel and I hope they can come back stronger than ever.

SWE at Intel?

I interned at Intel Asia R&D in Shanghai during the COVID-19 crisis, with my job description as “Android Security Engineer”. Chip development is exclusive to continental US and Israel due to protecting US chip secrets. Shanghai R&D is almost strictly software engineering.

Job description

I contributed to building product demos and adding functions to existing tools.

Tech stack?

C, shell, basic knowledge about virtualization tech. (I really didn’t have any)

My projects

Triple-OS demo

I created a product demo that utilized Intel’s gvt-g virtualization technology to show case the ability to run Ubuntu (host), Windows(VM), Android(VM) concurrently. The virtual graphics that gvt-g offers gives boosted graphics performance for VMs over traditional virtio implementations.

vm-manager

A command line vm-manager program that is written in C and shell. The program is programmed in a secure manner and allows users to create, update and start civ clients (android vm). The project is part of Project Celadon’s open source repo on github.

Internship experience

The people

My manager is a talkative and open person. He speaks his mind, both complements and criticisms. The engineer who I was assigned to was very nice, and taught me things when I get stuck. And I got stuck quite a few times.

COVID

I worked remotely for most of the 7-month Internship. I’m also enrolled at school and participated in various extra-curricular activitives. My manager and engineer was very considerate in allowing me to kind of work “part-time”, as long as I meet deadlines and deliver results.
